Casablanca
The White City

Morocco's largest metropolis. A sprawling, energetic hub where grand Art Deco architecture meets the towering majesty of the Hassan II Mosque above the Atlantic waves.

🏙️ Largest The biggest city in the Maghreb region and the economic capital of Morocco
🕌 210 m The height of the Hassan II Mosque minaret (one of the tallest in the world)
🎬 1942 The year the famous Hollywood film 'Casablanca' was released (shot entirely in studios)
🏛️ Mauresque Famous for a unique architectural blend of traditional Moorish and French Art Deco
Heritage

A Masterclass in
Modernity

Originally an ancient Berber settlement called Anfa, the city was destroyed by an earthquake in 1755. It was later rebuilt by Sultan Mohammed ben Abdallah, who renamed it "Dar al-Baida" (The White House).


During the 20th century French protectorate, European architects used Casablanca as a canvas. They developed the "Mauresque" style—a seamless blend of cutting-edge Art Deco geometry and traditional Moroccan zellige, carved stucco, and cedar. Today, it stands as a monument to cosmopolitan progress.

Monuments & Magic

Casablanca demands you look up, whether at towering minarets or sweeping concrete curves.

Hassan II Mosque

Built partially out over the ocean, this architectural titan took 10,000 artisans seven years to build. It is one of the few mosques in Morocco open to non-Muslim visitors.

The Habous Quarter

Also known as the Nouvelle Medina, built in the 1930s by the French. It is a picturesque, idealized version of a traditional medina with beautiful archways and neat markets.

Mohammed V Square

The administrative center of the city, surrounded by imposing Mauresque public buildings, including the Palace of Justice, the French Consulate, and the Grand Post Office.

La Corniche

The breezy, palm-lined beachfront promenade in the affluent Ain Diab neighborhood. It is packed with luxury beach clubs, cafes, and locals strolling at sunset.
