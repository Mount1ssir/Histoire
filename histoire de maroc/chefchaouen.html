Chefchaouen
The Blue Pearl

Nestled in the Rif Mountains, this serene city paints its winding streets in every shade of blue, creating a peaceful, dream-like atmosphere unlike anywhere else in the world.

⛰️ 1471 Founded as a small fortress to fight the Portuguese
💙 Blue Introduced by Jewish refugees in the 1930s to symbolize the sky and heaven
🐐 Rif The mountain range famous for its fresh goat cheese and woolen blankets
📸 Top 10 Frequently ranked as one of the most photogenic cities on earth
Heritage

An Andalusian
Retreat

Chefchaouen’s unique charm is heavily influenced by the Moorish and Jewish exiles from Andalusia (Spain) who settled here in the 15th century. They brought with them the red-tiled roofs, the central courtyards, and the distinct Andalusian architectural style that defines the town.


Life here moves at a mountain pace. The air is crisp, the medina is quiet, and the local artisans specialize in crafts not found elsewhere in Morocco, particularly woven woolen garments (like the traditional Djellaba) and vibrant woven blankets.

Discover

Monuments & Magic

Exploring Chefchaouen is about wandering aimlessly, but keep an eye out for these landmarks.

The Kasbah

A heavily restored walled fortress in the center of town. It contains a beautiful Andalusian-style garden and a small ethnographic museum.

The Spanish Mosque

Sitting on a hill overlooking the town, this ruined mosque is a 30-minute hike away and offers the most spectacular panoramic sunset views of the blue city.

Plaza Uta el-Hammam

The bustling main square lined with cafes and restaurants. A perfect spot to sip mint tea, listen to the call to prayer, and watch the world go by.

Ras El Maa

The waterfall and natural spring just outside the medina walls where locals gather to wash clothes and cool off in the mountain water.
