Meknes
The Versailles of Morocco

Shaped by the grand vision of a single Sultan, Meknes is a city of monumental gates, sprawling royal stables, and a quieter, more intimate soul than its famous neighbor, Fes.

👑 17th Century — The golden age when Sultan Moulay Ismail made it his capital
🧱 40 km The total length of the massive defensive walls surrounding the old city
🐎 12,000 The number of royal Arabian horses that were kept in the Sultan's legendary stables
🍇 Surrounded by Morocco's richest agricultural plains and vineyards
Heritage

The Dream of
Moulay Ismail

While founded in the 11th century by the Almoravids as a military settlement, Meknes was entirely transformed by the Alaouite Sultan Moulay Ismail (1672–1727). He envisioned a capital that could rival the grandeur of Louis XIV's Versailles.


He ordered the construction of a vast imperial city within the city, complete with immense granaries, underground prisons, and intricately tiled gates. Today, Meknes offers a far more relaxed and accessible medina experience than Fes, rewarding those who take the time to wander its historic limits.

Monuments & Magic

The landmarks of Meknes are built on a colossal scale, showcasing the might of the Alaouite dynasty.

Bab Mansour

Widely considered the most beautiful gate in North Africa. It is famous for its intricate zellige mosaics and massive marble columns looted from the nearby Roman ruins of Volubilis.

Heri es-Souani

The awe-inspiring royal granaries and stables. This massive complex, designed with brilliant ancient cooling systems, once housed massive reserves of grain and up to 12,000 horses.

Mausoleum of Moulay Ismail

The final resting place of the great Sultan. It is one of the few religious shrines in Morocco open to non-Muslims, featuring stunning stucco work, cedar ceilings, and a quiet courtyard.

Volubilis (Nearby)

Located just 30 minutes away, these are the best-preserved Roman ruins in North Africa. Famous for its intact, vibrant mosaic floors and grand triumphal arches standing in fields of wildflowers.
