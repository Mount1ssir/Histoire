Essaouira
The Windy City

A fortified coastal town renowned for its strong trade winds, historic brass cannons, and rich musical heritage. Essaouira is a laid-back sanctuary of art and ocean views.

🌳 Argan Located in the heart of Morocco's endemic Argan tree region
Heritage

Designed for
Harmony

Unlike other Moroccan medinas that grew organically over centuries, Essaouira was carefully designed in the 18th century. Sultan Sidi Mohammed ben Abdallah hired a French architect, Théodore Cornut, to build a modern fortress city that blended European military architecture with North African aesthetics.


This resulted in wide, straight streets inside thick, impenetrable ramparts. The city became a crucial hub where goods from sub-Saharan caravan routes were loaded onto European ships. Today, its relaxed vibe attracts artists, surfers, and musicians.

Discover

Monuments & Magic

The sea salt coats every surface in Essaouira, adding character to these historic spots.

Skala de la Ville

The dramatic sea bastion built along the cliffs. Lined with 18th-century European brass cannons, it offers spectacular views of the crashing Atlantic waves. (Famous as a filming location for Game of Thrones).

The Fishing Port

A chaotic, colorful, and highly photogenic working port. Hundreds of traditional wooden boats painted in signature Essaouira blue crowd the harbor while seagulls circle above.

The Medina

A UNESCO World Heritage site featuring bright white walls, blue doors, and a distinct lack of motorized vehicles. It is famous for its intricate Thuya woodcarving workshops.

Îles Purpuraires

A group of small islands just off the coast. In antiquity, they were used to produce Tyrian purple dye. Today, they are a protected sanctuary for the rare Eleonora's falcon.
