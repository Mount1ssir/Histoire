Rabat
The City of Light

Morocco’s capital elegantly bridges the ancient and the modern. Overlooking the Atlantic, Rabat is a city of sweeping boulevards, palm trees, and profound historical depth.

👑 1912 The year Rabat became the modern capital of Morocco
🏛️ UNESCO The entire city is listed as a World Heritage site for its blend of ancient and modern
🌊 Bou Regreg The river that separates Rabat from its twin city, Salé
🌴 Green Known for its vast gardens and eco-friendly urban initiatives
Heritage

Where Empires
Meet the Ocean

Founded in the 12th century by the Almohads, Rabat was envisioned as a massive imperial capital and a staging point for military campaigns into Spain. This grand ambition left behind monumental ruins like the Hassan Tower and the Kasbah of the Udayas.


Today, Rabat is the political and administrative heart of Morocco. It is noticeably calmer and cleaner than other major cities, offering a refined atmosphere where tree-lined avenues of French colonial architecture seamlessly merge with ancient Islamic ruins.

Monuments & Magic

Rabat’s landmarks are grand, open, and tied to the sea.

Kasbah of the Udayas

A tranquil fortress overlooking the ocean. Inside, you'll find winding blue-and-white streets reminiscent of Chefchaouen, and a beautiful Andalusian garden.

The Chellah

A hauntingly beautiful medieval fortified necropolis built on top of an ancient Roman city. Today, it is a serene ruin reclaimed by nature and populated by storks.

Hassan Tower

An ambitious 12th-century minaret for a mosque that was never finished. It stands surrounded by 200 columns and directly opposite the modern Mausoleum of Mohammed V.

Mausoleum of Mohammed V

A masterpiece of modern Alaouite architecture containing the tombs of the Moroccan king and his two sons. It features pristine white marble and exquisite zellige tilework.
