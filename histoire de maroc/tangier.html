Tangier
The Gateway

Where Africa looks across at Europe, and the Atlantic Ocean meets the Mediterranean Sea. Tangier is an intoxicating blend of cultures, artists, and maritime history.

🗺️ 14 km The distance across the Strait of Gibraltar to Spain
🌐 Zone Functioned as an International Zone administered by several countries until 1956
🌊 Two Oceans meet here: The Atlantic and the Mediterranean
🎨 Beat A famous sanctuary for the "Beat Generation" of writers and artists
Heritage

A Magnet for
Dreamers & Spies

Because of its strategic location, Tangier has been fought over by Phoenicians, Romans, Vandals, Arabs, Portuguese, British, and French. Its most famous era, however, was in the mid-20th century when it became an "International Zone."


During this time, the city became a playground for eccentric millionaires, secret agents, and visionary artists. Figures like Paul Bowles, William S. Burroughs, Henri Matisse, and the Rolling Stones were drawn to its brilliant light, bohemian cafes, and border-town freedom.

Discover

Monuments & Magic

Tangier looks outward. Its best landmarks offer views across the water and deep into history.

Cap Spartel

The exact point where the Atlantic Ocean and Mediterranean Sea meet. A beautiful 19th-century lighthouse marks this dramatic, wave-battered promontory.

Caves of Hercules

Legend says the Roman god Hercules rested here before his 11th labor. The cave’s sea-facing opening is famously shaped exactly like the map of Africa.

The Grand Socco

The grand square that connects the modern city (Ville Nouvelle) to the old Medina. A palm-fringed hub of activity where women in traditional Rif hats sell fresh produce.

The Kasbah

Sitting atop the highest point in Tangier, the old Sultan's palace (Dar el Makhzen) now houses a museum. The views from the Kasbah walls out to Spain are unforgettable.
