Heritage

The Crucible of
Intellect & Art

Fes is the cultural and spiritual anchor of Morocco. It reached its golden age in the 13th and 14th centuries under the Marinid dynasty, who built the exquisite madrasas (schools) that still stand today, covered in breathtaking stucco, cedar wood, and iconic blue Zellige tiles.


To walk through Fes el-Bali is to step back in time. The air smells of cedar, spices, and the heavy, earthy scent of the ancient tanneries. Mules carry goods through narrow passageways, and master craftsmen practice trades handed down through unbroken family lines.

Monuments & Magic

The Medina hides its treasures well. Here are the masterpieces you must seek out.

Chouara Tannery

Operating since the 11th century, these iconic stone vessels hold natural dyes (like poppy and saffron) used to treat leather exactly as it was done a millennium ago.

Bou Inania Madrasa

A Marinid architectural masterpiece featuring a stunning green-tiled minaret, intricately carved cedar, and some of the finest zellige tilework in the country.

Bab Bou Jeloud (The Blue Gate)

The grand entrance to the old medina. The tiles on the outside are Fassi blue (representing the city), while the tiles on the inside are Islamic green.

Al Quaraouiyine

Founded by a woman, Fatima al-Fihri. It is recognized by UNESCO and Guinness World Records as the oldest existing, continually operating educational institution in the world.
