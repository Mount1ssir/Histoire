Ouarzazate
The Door of the Desert

Set against the dramatic backdrop of the High Atlas Mountains, Ouarzazate is a city of stunning earthen kasbahs and the cinematic "Hollywood of Africa."

🏜️ 1,160 m The high altitude of the city, sitting on a bare plateau south of the High Atlas
🎬 Studios Home to Atlas Studios, one of the largest film studio complexes in the world
🏰 Kasbahs The starting point for the famous "Route of a Thousand Kasbahs"
☀️ Noor Home to the Ouarzazate Solar Power Station, one of the largest on Earth
Heritage

Caravans to
Cinemas

For centuries, Ouarzazate was a quiet, remote crossing point for African traders making the arduous journey toward northern Morocco and Europe. The surrounding valleys are studded with ancient *ksours* (fortified villages) built entirely from the red earth upon which they stand.


In the 20th century, the city found a new life. Its reliable sunlight, sweeping landscapes, and authentic ancient architecture drew international filmmakers. Today, Ouarzazate embraces its dual identity as the guardian of traditional Berber desert culture and a global cinematic powerhouse.

"The sky is wider here, the earth is redder, and history feels like it was written in the dust just yesterday."
Discover

Monuments & Magic

From ancient trading posts to abandoned movie sets, Ouarzazate blurs the line between history and fiction.

Aït Benhaddou

Located 30km from the city, this breathtaking *ksar* is a UNESCO World Heritage site and the most famous earthen village in Morocco. It has served as the backdrop for *Gladiator*, *Game of Thrones*, and *Lawrence of Arabia*.

Taourirt Kasbah

Situated right in the center of town, this massive 19th-century fortress was once the palace of the powerful Glaoui family. Its maze-like interior features incredibly well-preserved painted cedar ceilings and stucco.

Atlas Film Studios

Wander through colossal, surreal abandoned movie sets scattered across the desert. You can stand in a replica Egyptian temple, an ancient Roman arena, or a Tibetan monastery.

Fint Oasis

A lush, hidden green paradise located just a short drive from the dry city. It is a stunning contrast to the desert, where date palms and local villages cluster around a winding river.
