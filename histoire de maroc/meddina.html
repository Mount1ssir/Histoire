The Silver City

Tiznit

Famed for its silver jewelry and ancient ramparts, Tiznit offers a quiet, authentic look at Moroccan craftsmanship. The city's red-walled medina is a labyrinth of tradition where every piece of silver tells a story of Berber heritage.

The Blue Pearl

Safi

Perched on the Atlantic coast, Safi is Morocco's ancient city of clay and fire. Its hillside pottery quarter — the Colline des Potiers — fills the air with the scent of kilns burning since the 16th century. Here, master craftsmen shape earth into art, producing the vibrant ceramics that colour Moroccan tables across the world.

The Sahara Gate

Merzouga

Where the road ends and the dunes of Erg Chebbi begin. Merzouga is the gateway to the Sahara, offering golden sunrises, camel treks across rolling sands, and nights spent under a blanket of infinite stars.
